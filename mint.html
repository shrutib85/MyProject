MINT

Mint is considered one of the essential herbs in Ayurveda. Due to its various advantages, it’s a must-have herb for every home. It can help you fight stress, anxiety and depression and is also suitable for headaches and insomnia. It helps to boost your brain power, improve digestion and much more.


Mint is called Pudina in Sanskrit. It is a hardy perennial famous for its fragrance. The aromatic menthol wakes up the senses and decongest the orifices of the head.

It is also calming and relaxing for the digestion. There are many varieties, spearmint and peppermint are the two common ones.

How to use ?
  • Make a mint tea – add the leaves to boiling water and steep for 5-10 minutes

  • Add it to your room temperature drinking water (best to leave it overnight)

  • use mint infused water as a mist for your skin (fill it in a spray bottle and keep in the fridge)

  • Use to gargle and rinse your mouth


Benefits
  • Aids digestion, reduces gas, indigestion and irritable bowel

  • Improves oral health by reducing harmful bacterial growth in the mouth.

  • decongestant, great to use for steam inhalation.

  • Treatment for cough and cold, throat irritation

  • Cooling effect for your skin, useful for insect bites anti inflammatory

Precaution
  • Pudina tea might increase kidney damage in people, so it’s best to avoid large intake of pudina

  • Pudina tea might increase liver damage. So, using large amounts of pudina tea might worsen liver disease
