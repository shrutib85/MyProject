Hing

Hing (asafoetida) is used as a flavouring agent in food and as a traditional remedy for a variety of ailments. Asafoetida (Ferula asafoetida) is an oleo-gum-resin made from the stems of Ferula plants of the Umbelliferae family. Ferula plants are extensively spread in central Asia, particularly in west Afghanistan, Iraq, Turkey and Eastern Iran, Europe, and North Africa, with about 170 species.


Asafoetida is a spice which can be used in the dishes. Apart from being used as a spice, this herb is also used in the treatment of several chronic diseases such as asthma, epilepsy, stomach-ache, flatulence, intestinal parasites, weak digestion and influenza.

How to use ?
  • Oral consumption of hot water extract of the dried gum is used to treat hysteria, whooping cough, and ulcers.

  • Hingvastaj Churna or powder is a potent digestive remedy. The powder has digestive and carminative properties

  • Hing Tailam or Oil is highly significant in the treatment of flatulence, stomach problems, impotency, dental problems, cough, cold and impotency.

Benefits
  • Provides Relief from Respiratory Troubles.

  • Good for Cardiac Health.

  • Dismisses Headache.

  • Improves Digestion.

Precaution
  • Children should not be given Hing medication since it may cause methaemoglobinaemia.

  • Asafoetida consumption is contraindicated during pregnancy or lactation.

  • Avoid large uses of hing as it can cause mouth swelling, digestive problem.
