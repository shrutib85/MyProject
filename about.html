Research and Modernization:

Ongoing research aims to integrate Ayurvedic practices with modern scientific under standing to enhance its accep tance and effectiveness globally.

Global Reach:

Ayurveda has gained popularity beyond India and is practiced in many countries worldwide, often in complementary and integrative healthcare settings.

Core Principles:

Central to Ayurveda is the belief in three primary bio-energetic forces called doshas—Vata, Pitta, and Kapha—that govern an individual's physiological and psychological functions.

Diagnostic Techniques:

Ayurvedic practitioners use various methods like pulse diagnosis (Nadi Pariksha), observation, questioning, and examination to assess an individual's dosha balance.

Balance & Harmony:

Ayurveda seeks to achieve balance and harmony within the body, mind, and spirit . Imbalances in doshas are believed to lead to diseases and health issues.

Historical Origins:

Ayurveda has roots dating back over 5,000 years in the Indian subcontinent. It was passed down orally through generations before being documented in ancient texts.

Preventative and Holistic:

Ayurveda emphasizes preventative healthcare through a balanced lifestyle and wellness practices and wellness practices.

Integration with Modern Medicine:
